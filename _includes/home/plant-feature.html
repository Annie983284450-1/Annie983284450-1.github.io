Multi-task Computer Vision
for Plant Growth

A ConvLSTM encoder–decoder that jointly forecasts plant growth at the pixel level and classifies environmental conditions (drought vs. control). Domain-specific losses — difference and centroid — enforce temporal and geometric consistency in leaf shape and movement across frames.

The core methodological contribution is POI-based bi-polar regression: a physically meaningful evaluation metric that counts leaf growth vs. shrinkage at the pixel level, complementing SSIM/MSE. We benchmarked against ConvLSTM, PredRNN, PhyDNet and SimVP on the KOMATSUNA dataset, with consistent gains in rank-consistency.
