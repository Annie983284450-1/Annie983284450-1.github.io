Multi-task Computer Vision
for Plant Growth

The CAMP framework — Classification-assisted Multi-task Prediction — uses a PredRNN-based spatiotemporal memory to encode sequences of plant images, then splits into two heads: a frame-prediction branch that reconstructs future growth, and a label-prediction branch that classifies environmental conditions (drought vs. control). Kernel sharing between the two heads lets visual and categorical signals inform each other.

I also introduced POI-based bi-polar regression — a physically meaningful evaluation metric that counts leaf growth vs. shrinkage at the pixel level — complementing SSIM/MSE. Benchmarked against ConvLSTM, PredRNN, PhyDNet and SimVP on the KOMATSUNA dataset.
