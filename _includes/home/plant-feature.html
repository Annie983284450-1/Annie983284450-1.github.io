Methodology, Portable · NC State · Summer 2025

Computer Vision
for Plant Growth

Same instinct, different inputs: the CAMP framework — Classification-assisted Multi-task Prediction — uses PredRNN spatiotemporal memory to encode sequences of plant images, then splits into two heads — a frame-prediction branch that reconstructs future growth and a label-prediction branch that classifies environmental conditions (drought vs. control). Kernel sharing lets the two signals inform each other.

I also introduced POI-based bi-polar regression, a physically meaningful evaluation metric that counts leaf growth vs. shrinkage at the pixel level — complementing SSIM/MSE where those metrics miss the biology. Benchmarked against ConvLSTM, PredRNN, PhyDNet and SimVP on the KOMATSUNA and Arabidopsis datasets.
