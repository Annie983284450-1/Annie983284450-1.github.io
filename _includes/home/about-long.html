About

I'm Anni Zhou. My research has a single animating question: how do we build machine-learning systems that are honest about what they don't know? I bring tools from uncertainty quantification, conformal prediction, contextual bandits and reinforcement learning to problems where confidence matters — and I'm deliberately domain-curious about where those methods land next.

My Ph.D. at Georgia Tech (advised by Dr. Raheem Beyah and Dr. Rishikesan Kamaleswaran) traced a single arc in clinical decision support — from online expert ensembles (OnAI-Comp) through gap-based bandits with calibrated intervals (Sepsyn-OLCP) to neural contextual bandits with conformal LCB bonuses (NeuroSep-CP-LCB). The common thread isn't sepsis; it's trustworthy sequential decision-making.

The methods I work on — calibrated uncertainty, safe exploration, interpretable selection — travel. They were developed in the ICU. They're not stuck there.

Over summer 2025 I moved the toolkit to computer vision in agriculture with Dr. Yuchen Liu at NC State — the CAMP framework jointly forecasts plant growth frames and classifies environmental conditions, with a new POI-based bipolar-regression metric for physically meaningful evaluation. Different inputs, same instinct: design models that know when they shouldn't be trusted.

I'm open to the next domain: biomechanics, energy systems, earth observation, whatever has a real decision and a real cost when the model is wrong.
