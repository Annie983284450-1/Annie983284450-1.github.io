About

I'm Anni Zhou — I earned my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Georgia Tech in May 2025, advised by Dr. Raheem Beyah and Dr. Rishikesan Kamaleswaran. My research builds trustworthy, uncertainty-aware machine learning for clinical decision-making — with an emphasis on early sepsis prediction in the ICU, where a prediction without a calibrated confidence interval isn't safe to act on.

My dissertation threads three contributions into one pipeline: OnAI-Comp (a peer-reviewed online framework where heterogeneous ML experts compete for the right to predict the next patient), Sepsyn-OLCP (the same pipeline wrapped in split conformal prediction and a gap-based online selector), and NeuroSep-CP-LCB (a neural contextual bandit with an LCB bonus calibrated by conformal prediction, for safe offline policy learning).

A prediction without a calibrated confidence interval is not safe to act on.

Through summer 2025 I worked with Dr. Yuchen Liu at NC State on multi-task computer vision for agriculture: a ConvLSTM encoder–decoder that jointly forecasts plant growth at the pixel level and classifies environmental conditions (drought vs. control). The project introduced a novel Pixel-of-Interest (POI) bi-polar regression metric for physically meaningful evaluation, outperforming ConvLSTM, PredRNN, PhyDNet and SimVP on the KOMATSUNA dataset.
