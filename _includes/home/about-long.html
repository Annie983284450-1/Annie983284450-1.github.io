About

I'm Anni Zhou. My research has one animating question: how do we build machine-learning systems that are honest about what they don't know? I bring tools from uncertainty quantification, conformal prediction, contextual bandits and reinforcement learning to problems where confidence matters — and I'm deliberately domain-curious about where those methods land next.

My Ph.D. at Georgia Tech (advised by Dr. Raheem Beyah and Dr. Rishikesan Kamaleswaran) threaded three papers into one pipeline — the thesis context was sepsis prediction, but the common thread is trustworthy sequential decision-making. Over summer 2025 I moved the toolkit to computer vision in agriculture at NC State with Dr. Yuchen Liu. Different inputs, same instinct.

The methods were developed in the ICU. They're not stuck there.

I'm open to the next domain — biomechanics, energy systems, earth observation, whatever has a real decision and a real cost when the model is wrong.
