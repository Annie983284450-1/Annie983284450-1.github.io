About

I'm Anni Zhou — I earned my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Georgia Tech in May 2025, advised by Dr. Raheem Beyah and Dr. Rishikesan Kamaleswaran. My research builds trustworthy, uncertainty-aware machine learning for clinical decision-making, with an emphasis on early sepsis prediction.

My thesis — Trustworthy and Robust Early Sepsis Prediction for Intensive Care Unit Patients using Reinforcement Learning and Conformal Prediction — threads together online learning, contextual bandits, and split conformal prediction to give calibrated, actionable confidence bounds at the bedside. Recent work includes Sepsyn-OLCP and NeuroSep-CP-LCB, both released as preprints in March 2025 with open-source code.

Through summer 2025 I worked with Dr. Yuchen Liu at NC State on multi-task computer vision for agriculture — ConvLSTM and encoder–decoder architectures for plant-growth prediction with domain-specific losses on the KOMATSUNA dataset.
